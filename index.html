When Deterministic Business Meets Probabilistic AI

AI doesn't fix broken organizations — it amplifies them. Before chasing AI value, diagnose your real constraints.

🧠 Human-Level Constraints

  1. Trust Would your team see AI as a tool — or a threat?
  2. Skills Can your average employee write an effective business prompt today?
  3. Politics Would any manager block AI to protect their control?
  4. Accountability When AI gets it wrong — clear path, or everyone freezes?

⚙️ System-Level Constraints

  1. Data Can AI access your data without a manual spreadsheet export?
  2. Process Automate your top workflow — good results faster or bad results faster?
  3. Compliance Does legal know what AI agents are allowed to do?
  4. Integration How many systems need credentials before an agent is useful?
"You can't fix a broken house with a faster vacuum cleaner."

Strategic Pillars for AI Adoption

Four pillars, four different games — each with different scope, risk, and impact.

Pillar 1
Productivity AI
Chat tools, MS Office AI, Copilots, productivity helpers
Quick Win Low Risk

"We use AI"

Pillar 2
Remodelling AI
Transform new/existing business processes
Medium Term Medium Risk

"AI runs our ops"

Pillar 3
Development AI
Remodel the SDLC and how software is built
Continuous Foundational

"AI builds our AI"

Pillar 4
Big Ideas AI
Fundamentally change how the org operates in its industry
Long Term High Risk

"AI IS our product"

Key Insight

Pillar 1 is table stakes. If your AI strategy stops at productivity tools, you're funding innovation for your competitors.
